Cultural Cities of Afghanistan

City Gallery

Afghanistan is a country in the heart of Asia with a temperate climate and diverse people and cultures.

Badakhshan

One of the 34 provinces of Afghanistan, located in the northeast. Badakhshan is 1,800 meters above sea level and its capital is the city of Faizabad. The province shares international borders with Tajikistan to the north, China to the east, and Pakistan to the south. Badakhshan is the most densely populated province in Afghanistan, with 28 districts.

Ghor

Ghor is famous as an ancient city with the tallest minaret.Most of the people of this province speak Persian and Dari. The inhabitants of this province are Tajiks, Hazaras, Aimaqs, Uzbeks, Pashtuns, etc.The Jam Tower is located 60 kilometers northeast of Shahrak County in the village called Jam.

Kapisa

The history of Kapisa dates back to the era of the Kushan emperor Kanishka the Great and also to the Buddhist era of Western Greece, when Kapisa was chosen as Kanishka's summer capital at that time. Among the historical areas of this province, one can mention the area called Khamzargar in the Hesa-Awal district of Kohistan, where most of the historical monuments are also found.

Baghlan is known as an industrial and agricultural city.Baghlani's pain has a special taste and is well-known throughout the country. Its other products include potatoes, onions, corn, and chickpeas, which also have delicious and diverse fruits.

Panjshir

Panjshir, known for its lush and fertile valley and precious gems such as the famous emerald, is located in central Afghanistan.

Kunduz

Kunduz is known as an ancient and historic city with a centuries-old historical citadel.
