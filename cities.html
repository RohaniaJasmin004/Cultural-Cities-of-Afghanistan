City of the Day

Parwan

The word Parwan in Avesta comes from the Dise Purana and means a wall, a passage, and a valley. It also means a spinning silk wheel that is turned by foot. Of course, Parwan is the same area that was named Jabal al-Saraj during the time of Amir Habibullah Siraj after the establishment of a power dam and a royal palace, and today it is one of the cities of Parwan province. It has been said in the world: Parwan is a city with blessings and a place for merchants and the gateway to India. Upian, Bagram, Ghorband, and Salang are also among its ancient regions.

  • Gul Ghundi is one of the famous and recreational areas in the center of this province, whose purple flowers have been praised by poets.
  • Parwan is one of the largest producers of grapes and raisins in the country.
  • Parwan also has a power plant and a factory.
